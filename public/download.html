EnigmaLink - private sending

EnigmaLink sends documents or pictures safely from any device to any device. Upload a document to encrypt it onto your Google Drive, then share a link to your document via email, text or twitter. Documents are encrypted with a state-of-the-art AES hardware encryption and stored in your own Google Drive account. Neither Enigma Bridge nor any prying eyes can see your documents without the links (or QR codes) that are generated by your own browser. Each document is protected with at leat two digital locks:
  • Strong encryption key 1 - (AES-256) that is part of the link you actually share - this protects against attacks originating from your network (e.g., an insecure WiFi) or from any third party.
  • Strong encryption key 2 - (AES-256) encrypted by the Enigma Bridge cloud HSM service. Once encrypted, this key is stored with the actual file you want to share. Enigma Bridge is a service that uses processers with independent validations of physical security. It means that keys inside those processors can't be extracted.
  • Password (optional) - your optional password is combined with other keys in such a way that any attempt to guess it have to include a call to the Enigma Bridge service. This also means that an internal counter of operations is incremented and you are able to detect any unexpected requests to decrypt your document. (The counting mechanism is not available through currently implemented EnigmaLink interfaces. Still, this design very much protects from brute-force attacks.)
